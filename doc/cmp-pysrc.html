  8  """Compare the predicted tags to the original tags. 
  9   
 10  The data of the file must be formated in a particular way: the CoNLL 2002 
 11  format. The data consists of two columns separated by a single space. Each 
 12  word has been put on a separate line and there is an empty line after each 
 13  sentence. The first item on each line is a word and the second the named 
 14  entity tag. The tags have the same format as in the chunking task: a B 
 15  denotes the first item of a phrase and an I any non-initial word. There are 
 16  four types of phrases: person names (PER), organizations (ORG), locations 
 17  (LOC) and miscellaneous names (MISC). Here is an example: 
 18  C{        Wolff B-PER} 
 19  C{            , O} 
 20  C{    currently O} 
 21  C{            a O} 
 22  C{   journalist O} 
 23  C{           in O} 
 24  C{    Argentina B-LOC}

 32  C{        final O} 
 33  C{        years O} 
 34  C{           of O} 
 35  C{          the O} 
 36  C{    seventies O} 
 37  C{           in O} 
 38  C{         Real B-ORG} 
 39  C{       Madrid I-ORG} 
 40  C{            . O} 
 41   
 42  These functions compare the named entities predicted by the program to the 
 43  original named entities of the dataset. The precision column is the
